Acacia Tortilis

السمر

Basic Information

Acacia Tortilis, also known as the Umbrella Thorn Acacia, is a hardy tree native to the Middle East and Africa. It's well adapted to dry, desert conditions and is highly valued for its shade and resilience in arid landscapes.

  • Type: Tree
  • Lifespan: 30–50 years
  • Height: Up to 15 meters
  • Blooming Season: Spring

Care & Benefits

Acacia Tortilis is easy to grow in sandy, well-drained soil and is highly drought-tolerant. It provides excellent shade and supports desert wildlife.

  • Care Level: Easy
  • Benefits: Shade provider, drought-tolerant, supports biodiversity
  • Note: Some thorns may cause injury if not handled carefully

Growing Conditions

Sunlight: Full Sun

Climate: Hot and Dry

Temperature Tolerance: Up to 45°C, Frost-sensitive

Soil: Sandy, Well-drained

Placement: Outdoor only

Watering: Very low after establishment

Medicinal & Special Properties

The pods and leaves of Acacia Tortilis have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory benefits. Caution is needed with livestock as some parts may be mildly toxic if consumed in large amounts.

  • Medicinal Uses: Pain relief, anti-inflammation (traditional uses)
  • Toxicity: May affect livestock if overgrazed
